# Case Study: Phishing Attack Targeting College Students

## Type of Cybercrime: Phishing

## What is Phishing?
Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers send fake emails
or messages pretending to be from trusted organizations
to steal sensitive personal information like passwords,
OTPs, or bank details.

## How It Happens (Step by Step)

Step 1: The attacker creates a fake email that looks
exactly like it came from a real company such as a
job portal, bank, or university.

Step 2: The email creates urgency - for example,
"Your account will be blocked in 24 hours" or
"You have been selected for an internship - respond immediately."

Step 3: The victim clicks on a link in the email
that leads to a fake website designed to look real.

Step 4: The fake website asks the victim to enter
login credentials, personal details, or OTP.

Step 5: The attacker captures all entered information
and uses it to access real accounts or steal money.

## Who is Usually Targeted?
College students actively applying for internships
and jobs are prime targets. They are eager, act
quickly on opportunities, and may not verify sources
carefully before responding.

## Real Example (Fictional Scenario)
Ravi, a second-year engineering student, received an
email claiming he was shortlisted for a paid internship.
The email had a professional logo and urgent language.
He clicked the link and entered his email credentials
for "verification." Within hours, his email account
was compromised and used to send spam to his contacts.

## Consequences
- Identity theft and account compromise
- Financial loss if banking details were shared
- Mental stress and anxiety
- Loss of trust in genuine online opportunities
- Damage to personal and professional reputation
